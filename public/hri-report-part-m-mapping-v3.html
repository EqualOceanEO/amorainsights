⏱️ Supply Chain Convergence Timeline

中美供应链能力收敛的关键时间节点预测

2023-2024
Technology Validation Phase
Both sides demonstrate working prototypes. China focuses on hardware iteration speed, US on AI capabilities. Supply chains remain largely separate.
2025 (Current)
Pilot Deployment Phase
Unitree deploys 5500+ units globally; Tesla <50. China's manufacturing advantage becomes evident. US OEMs struggle with scale production.
2026-2027
Critical Inflection Point
China closes AI gap through data scale; US attempts manufacturing reshoring. Supply chain competition intensifies. Convergence begins.
2028+
Winner Takes Most
Whoever reaches 100K units first gains insurmountable cost advantage. Supply chain regionalization solidifies. Platform layer remains US-dominated.

🇨🇳 China's Supply Chain Resilience

1
Manufacturing Density Moat: Complete component ecosystem within Pearl River & Yangtze River Delta regions enables rapid iteration and cost reduction.
2
Actuator Dominance: 30+ domestic suppliers covering all categories from micro servos to high-torque joints, the most complete globally.
3
Speed Advantage: Unitree's 5500+ unit deployment in 2024 vs Tesla's <50 demonstrates execution gap in volume manufacturing.
4
Platform Gap: Still 2-3 years behind in AI training infrastructure and simulation platforms, but closing fast through data scale.

🇺🇸 US Supply Chain Vulnerabilities

1
Manufacturing Gap: No domestic actuator ecosystem; 70%+ dependency on Asian suppliers for critical components.
2
Platform Lock-in Risk: NVIDIA CUDA ecosystem creates 3-5 year moat but also concentration risk for US OEMs.
3
Scale Challenge: All US OEMs combined <100 units deployed vs Unitree alone at 5500+; manufacturing learning curve lag.
4
Geopolitical Exposure: Force sensors (ATI), precision gearboxes (Harmonic Drive) concentrated in single suppliers.
